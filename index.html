About Enactus

What is Enactus?

Enactus is a global community of university students, academics, and business leaders who use entrepreneurial action to tackle real social and environmental challenges. Student teams research problems in their communities, build sustainable solutions as mini-ventures, measure impact, and share results at national and global stages.

The meaning of “Enactus”

The name combines Entrepreneurial + Action + Us. It captures how students act like founders—identifying needs, building solutions, and delivering outcomes that benefit people and the planet.

How Enactus projects work

  1. Discover needs: research stakeholders, map root causes, define success metrics.
  2. Design a solution: a product/service and a basic business model.
  3. Validate: prototypes, user testing, quick pilots, iterate on feedback.
  4. Implement: launch a small venture with partners and beneficiaries.
  5. Measure: track KPIs (social, environmental, economic) and unit economics.
  6. Share & scale: present at Nationals and the Enactus World Cup; grow what works.

Why it matters

Enactus blends the speed of startups with the purpose of nonprofits. Students learn leadership, product thinking, finance, partnerships, and public speaking—while communities get solutions that last.

Global footprint

Enactus operates through independent country offices with university teams on each campus. Every season culminates in a national competition and the Enactus World Cup, where the most impactful projects compete on a global stage.

  • 33+ countries with Enactus offices
  • ~60 university teams in Egypt (season-dependent)

Featured Projects

Add your current ERU projects here.

ChitoRust

ChitoRust is a natural biocontrol solution developed from shrimp-shell chitosan to combat yellow rust disease in wheat, one of Egypt’s most critical agricultural threats. Unlike traditional chemical pesticides, ChitoRust is biodegradable, safe for the environment, and enhances crop resistance naturally. By reducing chemical dependency, it protects farmers’ health, lowers costs, and contributes to sustainable food security.

ChitoCoat

ChitoCoat uses chitosan extracted from shrimp waste to create an edible, transparent coating applied to fruits and vegetables. This innovative solution slows down spoilage, reduces microbial growth, and extends shelf life without synthetic preservatives. ChitoCoat provides farmers and retailers with a low-cost, eco-friendly method to reduce food waste, preserve quality, and deliver healthier produce to consumers.

ChitoGuard

ChitoGuard is a bioactive wound dressing that incorporates chitosan’s natural healing and antimicrobial properties. The bandage accelerates tissue regeneration, minimizes infection risk, and creates a moist environment that speeds up recovery compared to conventional dressings. Designed to be cost-effective and accessible, ChitoGuard represents a leap forward in affordable healthcare and sustainable medical innovation.

AgriVolt

AgriVolt develops eco-friendly batteries by replacing up to 60% of the traditional materials with agricultural waste. This approach not only reduces battery costs but also cuts down on harmful mining and waste, creating a cleaner and more sustainable energy source. AgriVolt shows how farmers’ residues can power the future of renewable energy.
